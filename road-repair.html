🛣️ Road Repair Complaint Application

A Road Repair Complaint Application is used to report damaged roads, potholes, cracked pavements, uneven surfaces, waterlogging due to poor road conditions, or other issues affecting public safety and transportation.

📝 Application Format

To,
The Executive Engineer (Roads),
Municipal Corporation,
Delhi

Subject: Complaint Regarding Road Repair

Respected Sir/Madam,

I would like to bring to your notice the poor condition of a road in my locality. The details of the complaint are as follows:

  • Name of Complainant: ______________________
  • Address: ______________________
  • Mobile Number: ______________________
  • Location of Road: ______________________
  • Nature of Damage: Potholes / Cracks / Uneven Surface / Waterlogging / Other
  • Date Issue Noticed: ______________________

The damaged road is causing inconvenience to residents and may pose a risk to pedestrians and vehicles. I request the concerned department to inspect the site and carry out the necessary repair work.

Thanking You,

Yours faithfully,
______________________ (Complainant Name)
______________________ (Signature)
______________________ (Date)

📌 Guide: How to File a Road Repair Complaint

Step 1: Visit the Municipal Portal

Open the Municipal Corporation grievance portal and select the "Road Repair Complaint" service.

Step 2: Fill the Complaint Form

Enter your details and provide the exact location of the damaged road.

Step 3: Upload Supporting Evidence

  • Photographs of the Damaged Road
  • Location Details
  • Additional Remarks (if any)

Step 4: Submit the Complaint

Verify the information and submit the complaint online.

Step 5: Receive Complaint Number

A complaint reference number will be generated for tracking purposes.

Step 6: Inspection & Repair Work

The roads department may inspect the reported location and schedule repair or maintenance work as required.

⚠️ Important Tips

  • Provide the exact road location or nearby landmark.
  • Upload clear photographs showing the damage.
  • Report major hazards immediately.
  • Keep the complaint number safe for status tracking.
